# Math Warrior Game Online!

+ Rule Book
    + Game Overview
        + Math Warriors is a two-player strategy and arithmetic game that uses six different polyhedral dice per player. The aim is to capture all of your opponent's dice using either strength-based or math-based           attacks.
        + Math Warriors is a game that tests student’s ability to do mental sums as well as strategic thinking. There is also an element of luck involved which makes the game even more exciting.
        + Components
            + Each player gets one of each of the following dice:
                + D4 (Tetrahedron)
                + D6 (Cube)
                + D8 (Octahedron)
                + D10 (Decahedron)
                + D12 (Dodecahedron)
                + D20 (Icosahedron)
                + 1 Penalty Chip
                + Scoring Sheet
    + Setup
    1. Each player rolls all six of their dice.
    2. Arrange the dice in increasing numerical order based on the number shown on top.
    3. Compare the smallest die value of each player.
    The player with the lower number starts the game and receives the Penalty Chip.
    + Gameplay
    + Players take turns attempting to capture one of the opponent's dice using either:
        1. Strength Attack
        2. Mind Attack
            ● Strength Attack
            1. Choose one of your dice.
            2. You may capture an opponent's die if your die's value ≥ their die's value.
            3. Both dice are removed from play. The attacking die is re-rolled and kept in play.
            ● Mind Attack
            1. Use two or more of your dice.
            2. Use any combination of +, -, x, ÷ to match the exact value of one of your opponent's dice.
            If successful:
            1. The opponent's die is captured.
            2. All dice used in the attack are re-rolled.
            3. If you cannot make an exact match, your turn is skipped.
            ● Special Rules
            1. No Attack Possible: If you cannot perform any valid attack, you forfeit your turn.
            2. Final Capture Rule: If you were the first player to start the game, you must win the final capture with a higher value die.

    + Strategies Guide:
    
          + General Strategies
          Dice Management is Key: Since you only have one of each die, be mindful of which ones you use and lose. Losing your higher-value dice early may hurt your chances later.
          Balance Risk and Reward: Strength Attacks are safer but less creative. Mind Attacks carry more risk (you might fail) but can be more efficient and impressive if executed well.
          
          + Strength Attack Strategies
          Use High Dice on High Targets: Save your D20, D12, and D10 for high-value captures. Don't waste them on low-value targets unless necessary.
          Bait with Lower Dice: Leave a medium-value die exposed to tempt your opponent into attacking it, then retaliate using a better-positioned die.
          Reroll Advantage: Since attacking dice are rerolled and kept in play, sometimes it’s worth attacking just to get a better number on a die.
          
          + Mind Attack Strategies
          Maximize Low Dice: Smaller dice are perfect for Mind Attacks—they’re less valuable in Strength Attacks and can combine easily.
          Retain Your Highest Dice: Don’t use D20 or D12 in Mind Attacks unless absolutely necessary. They're better for brute-force Strength Attacks.
          Practice Mental Math Patterns: Quick recognition of sums like 6 + 8 = 14 or 12 ÷ 2 = 6 gives you an edge in faster decision-making.
          
          + Turn Order & Penalty Chip Strategies
          Use the First Turn Wisely: As the starting player, you’re at a disadvantage for the last capture (Final Capture Rule). Consider playing more aggressively to gain the upper hand early.
          Plan the Final Capture: Always try to keep one strong die for the endgame. If you started, you must win with a higher die—don’t waste your last big number too early.
          
          + Advanced Tactics
          Dice Tracking: Mentally keep track of what dice your opponent has left. Knowing they’ve lost their D20, for example, lets you plan safer Strength Attacks.
          Mind Games: Bluffing is possible. Appear to target one die but suddenly switch to another using a Mind Attack.
          Combo Setting: Sometimes it’s worth skipping a Strength Attack to reroll a die into a number you can use next turn in a Mind Attack combo.
